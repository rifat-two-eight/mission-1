Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, known for pioneering the concept of microfinance and establishing Grameen Bank. His innovative approach empowers the poor, especially women, by providing small loans to help them create sustainable businesses. Dr. Yunus's efforts have transformed the lives of millions and earned global recognition for his work in alleviating poverty, promoting social entrepreneurship, and advocating for financial inclusion and economic justice worldwide.
